About Flipped Classroom

The flipped classroom is an instructional strategy where traditional teaching methods are reversed. Instead of introducing new content during class time, students first learn new material at home through videos, readings, or other resources. Class time is then used for engaging in activities that deepen understanding, such as discussions, problem-solving, or hands-on exercises.

Key Features:

  • Pre-Class Learning: Students review lecture material or complete readings at home. This can be done through recorded video lectures, online resources, or assigned readings. Students can learn at their own pace, pausing and replaying content as needed.
  • In-Class Activities: Class time is dedicated to applying the material learned at home. This can include group work, projects, or interactive exercises. Emphasizes student engagement through discussions, problem-solving, and practical exercises.
  • Feedback and Assessment: Teachers can provide immediate feedback and support during class activities. Often includes online assessments or quizzes to evaluate understanding and retention of the material.

Benefits:

  • Enhanced understanding through active engagement and application of knowledge.
  • Increased engagement and interaction during class activities.
  • Flexibility for students to learn at their own pace and revisit content as needed.
  • More teacher interaction and personalized support.

Challenges:

  • Requires access to technology and internet resources for all students.
  • Teachers need to invest time in creating or curating high-quality pre-class materials.
  • Students need to be disciplined and take responsibility for their pre-class learning.
